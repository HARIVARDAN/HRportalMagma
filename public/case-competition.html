Case Competition

The concept of conducting case study competition is to know the ability of a participant in developing the best solution to a business or education-related case study within an allocated time frame, typically with teams of two or more individuals pitted against each other in a head-to-head or broader relative ranking.

Participants exercise skills and knowledge on a "real world" case for an actual organization, with the support of representatives who can provide professional advice.

Select an issue based on its degree of importance, and employ the competition as a means to both highlight the issue and create potential solutions through the efforts of the competitors.

Teams in case competitions are tasked with assessing the situation facing the organization, analyzing available information, crafting a solution, and defending their recommendations. In general, teams adhere to a time limit and specific rules. Each team/participant is judged independently.
Based upon the score in case competition th participants will be offered job interviews.

WHO CAN PARTICIPATE :
Participating team can be internal to a business school, or they can involve team from different schools
